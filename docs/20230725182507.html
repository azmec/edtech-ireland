Appendix

From Competence Centres to Technology Centres

The Competence Centre Programme continues to operate today under Enterprise and IDA Ireland but under a new name: Technology Centres. However, no explicit report of this name change published by either the DETE, EI, or IDA Ireland has been identified. At the programme’s genesis, EI reported expenditures and expansions to the programme in their Annual Report series of publications. In their 2007 report, for instance, they describe the program as affording Irish enterprises opportunities to, “engage in higher risk, longer-term research that will give them a competitive edge in international markets” (29). EI makes similar references to the program in 2008 and 2009 but not 2010 and onward, instead credited to the then-new Technology Centre Programme for affording enterprises opportunities to, “achieve the kind of transformational change required to develop new technologies and bring innovative products and services to market” (12). Further, Enterprise Ireland’s webpage for the program properly described the program in length in 17 December 2010 (CITE). By 16 April 2013, the page had been modified only to read “Technology Centres Initiative” rather than “Competence Centres Initiative” (CITE). This evidence is presented due to the absence of primary documents describing the transition.
